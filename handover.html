1. What this is and how it was built

Every wallet controlled by, or transacting with, the group was traced on-chain across Ethereum, Base and Arbitrum using Blockscout, then cross-referenced against (a) the team's Telegram payment confirmations and (b) Count's posted Xero subscriber set. Each wallet is named, classified into an accounting bucket, and carries a note explaining its role. On the Ethereum and Base perimeter, 99.79% of all stablecoin movement is identified; the residual is sub-threshold dust.

3. Accounting treatment by bucket

  • Internal / own — group operating wallets. Intra-group movement; not income or expense.
  • Registered counterparties — named investors (share premium / SAFT) and suppliers.
  • Own rails (exchange / bridge / DeFi) — Kraken, Gemini, LI.FI, Uniswap, Aave, MayanCircle, Permit2 etc. Pass-through infrastructure; excluded (funds are the group's own, routing).
  • Treasury / liquidity — Safes and BitGo. Excluded from P&L.
  • Unregistered — sub-threshold dust/fees only. Immaterial.

  • Figures are on-chain stablecoin movements (USDC, USDC.e, USDT, DAI) verified via Blockscout, cross-checked to Telegram timestamps to the minute.
  • Ethereum + Base perimeter is complete (99.79% identified). Arbitrum is folded in as its own clearly-scoped layer.
